About

My name is Oliver, and I'm from a small town towards the south of England called Flitwick. Ever since I was about 13-years-old, I have lived and breathed the web.

Now at 19-years-old, I reside in Leeds, England, where I'm studying in my third year at university for a degree in New Media – but I prefer to be a bit more precise with that declaration; what I actually study is front-end web development. Currently, I'm on a full-time placement at Clock, a web shop based just outside of London.

On most occassions, you will find me sat contemplating with a cup of tea in one hand, whilst I use the other to navigate up down a DOM tree. I'm never happier than when I'm knees deep into a project, learning something new. I thoroughly enjoy the challenge of building scalable, flexible and maintainable websites – not just your ordinary local school website, but large and complicated websites. I have adopted principles the likes of OOCSS and SMACSS for building my CSS, and I recently begun to use the Backbone.js framework for building JavaScript webapps.

I contribute code to GitHub, wherever I can. I post snippets of my thoughts on Twitter. Away from the computer, I take an interest in travelling, and some photography. All of my photos are available for viewing on Flickr.

I really believe that computers can improve lives. I bare witness to it every day. But I believe we need to make technology more human. As a web developer, I want to be a part of that revolution.
